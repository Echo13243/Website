Judaism

The Shield of David, a hexagram, was used in multiple cultures and civilizations, and was only associated with Judaism through 3 events.

It began with followers of Shabbetai Tsevi bearing an amulet. The amulet was a hexagram with the letters MBD, Mashiah ben David or "Messiah, son of David"

The symbol came to be associated with Judaism in the late 1700s, but was not accepted by Orthodox Jews.

The next two events cemented the symbol with Judaism. The first event was the Zionist movement‘s adoption of the symbol, where it appeared on the flag of Israel. In the 1930s to 1940s, Jews were forced to wear a yellow Shield of David with the word "Jude", or Jew, in all Nazi controlled lands.

The symbol stayed with Judaism throughout their hardship, and so was accepted into most Jewish places of worship and has become a positive symbol of Judaism.
