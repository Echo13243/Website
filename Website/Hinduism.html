Hinduism

One of the most sacred of Sanskrit (the primary sacred language of Hinduism) syllables is Om, or alternatively spelled Aum.

The syllable is used at the beginning and end of recitations and prayers, like "Amen".

The first chapter of one of the latest Vedic Upanishads, sacred Hindu writings, explains the syllable. It is divided into 4 parts, each representing the 4 states of mind. "A" is the awakened state, "U" is the dream state, "M" is the dreamless sleep, and the entire syllable is the turiya, beyond words and is itself the Ultimate, the brahman (the eternal creator and preserver of the universe).

In other Upanishad, imagery integrates Om into meditation. Om is the bow, and atman (self) is the arrow, and brahman is the target. "One must bend toward the target without diverting the mind; one must make oneself identical to the arrow."

Om stands with religious energy, constantly repeated in recitations, prayers, and sacred texts.
